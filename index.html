Clinical Context & Research Background

Key Research Findings: This calculator is based on validated nTMS excitability metrics from the King's College Hospital GBM cohort study (n=77 patients).

Cortical Excitability Score (CES): Higher scores indicate more abnormal interhemispheric excitability and are associated with worse survival (HR=2.52, p=0.031). 89.1% of patients showed abnormal excitability patterns.

IntraM1 Excitability Score (IMES): Lower scores reflect impaired intrahemispheric motor pathway integrity and correlate with poorer outcomes (HR=0.15, p=0.003).

Independent Predictors: These nTMS metrics remained significant even after adjusting for established prognostic factors (MGMT status, age, extent of resection), suggesting they capture unique tumor-brain interaction patterns.

Research Use Only: This model is based on research findings and should not replace clinical judgment or established prognostic tools in patient care. Results are for research and educational purposes only.
